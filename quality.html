Quality & compliance

This page is intended for US procurement, QA, and compliance review. It describes documentation scope and operational controls.

Summary

  • Batch-level documentation: COA and lot identifiers provided upon request.
  • Traceability: Chain-of-custody details shared where available for the lot.
  • Testing: Third-party laboratory testing documentation available by program.
  • Export paperwork: Commercial and customs documentation coordinated as needed.

Document availability and scope can vary by lot, source, and customer requirements.

Document request process

1) Requirements
Send your vendor packet and required testing panel.
2) NDA (if required)
NDA workflows are available prior to sharing controlled documents.
3) Review
We provide applicable documents for your internal review and qualification.

GMP manufacturing (context)

Material may be manufactured and handled in facilities that operate under GMP requirements. Program details are provided upon request.

Program-specific details

GMP documentation is shared upon request and may depend on supplier program and NDA.

No public certification claims

We do not claim certifications on this public site. Verification is handled through documentation review and your qualification process.

Customer responsibility

Your team determines supplier suitability through audits, SOPs, and internal review.

US regulatory notes (general)

This section is informational and not legal advice. Requirements depend on intended use, formulation, labeling, and distribution model.

Labeling & claims

  • No consumer claims: We do not provide consumer-facing claim language.
  • Customer-controlled labeling: Label content and claims are controlled by the brand owner/manufacturer.
  • Internal substantiation: Your team is responsible for substantiation and compliance review.

cGMP & supplier qualification

  • Supplier qualification: Managed by your SOPs (e.g., vendor packet, risk assessment, audits).
  • Documentation: Lot documentation and supporting records may be provided upon request, subject to availability and NDA.
  • Testing: Incoming testing requirements govern acceptance. Third-party results may be provided where available.

Batch-level COA & traceability

Shipments can be linked to lot documentation for receiving and recordkeeping.

COA

  • Lot identification: COA references lot/batch identifiers.
  • Methods: Test methods and laboratories are stated where applicable.
  • Scope: Attribute scope is defined by the program and your requirements.

COA formats vary by laboratory and supplier program. We can share a sample format on request.

Traceability

  • Chain-of-custody: Shared where available for the lot.
  • Country of origin: Provided per lot documentation.
  • Shipment linkage: Packing lists and lot identifiers aligned where applicable.

Traceability depth can vary by source; we disclose what is available for the lot.

Third-party laboratory testing

Testing panels are program-specific. Third-party laboratory reports and/or COA results can be provided upon request.

Identity

Botanical identification methods documented as applicable to the program.

Safety-related panels

Microbiological and contaminant results provided based on requirements.

Method & lab transparency

Laboratory and test method details provided where applicable.

Note: Testing scope, laboratories, and methods are confirmed per lot and program. Nothing on this page is a guarantee of specifications for a particular lot.

Export & documentation coordination

We coordinate paperwork required for import and receiving. Specific requirements depend on destination, carrier, and customer SOPs.

Common documents

  • Commercial invoice
  • Packing list
  • Certificate of origin (where required)
  • Harmonized code and customs descriptions as applicable

Additional documents (as applicable)

  • SDS (Safety Data Sheet)
  • Phytosanitary documentation (where required)
  • Allergen statement (where applicable)
  • Other customer forms per vendor packet
